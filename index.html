photography

Since I was a young teenager I always had a camera in my hands taking photos. During design school, we took photography classes and I got my first SLR, and learned how to take manual photos, develop film and print our own photos. Ever since then, whatever I see is in the form of a potential photo. Until the advent of digital photography, practice was limited by the number of rolls of film I could afford to develop and print. Now digital photography has opened up a whole new world, and my digital SLR is one of my most loved possessions. While travelling for a month through Belize, my partner and I actually carried three cameras around with us, all for different purposes. And now that my phone can take photos as well, I love that I am never without this tiny camera with me at all times. The beauty of the world seen through a series of photos.

cats

I come from a dog family. My grandparents, aunt and uncle, and my parents always had dogs – and I loved them. When my parent’s beagle Ginger passed away when I was around 4 or 5, I immediately wanted a CAT. So at a cottage garage sale, we adopted Suzy, and since we were a one pet household and Suzy lived to be over 20 years old, she was my only pet growing up, and started my love of cats. When I moved into my own house, I adopted Pekoe, an adult cat from a friend’s sister, then Tazo. When Pekoe passed away from cancer, I decided I’d like to foster cats in his honour, and so started my journey into cat rescue. I have volunteered with the Annex Cat Rescue for over ten years now, been on the board of directors and held many coordinator type positions, but fostering and feeding the feral colonies has always been my favourite. I don’t foster much anymore since I am now at my maximum of six cats (photos above!) Dutchie, Tazo, Sidney, Penny, Bunny and Tammy, but I continue to help out feeding the feral cats, which is a big problem in the city of Toronto.
